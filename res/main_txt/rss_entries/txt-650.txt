

            Nadia Schadlow, the White House deputy national security adviser for strategy, has resigned and will leave her position on April 27.Schadlow is the third national security official to resign or be pushed out after John Bolton became national security adviser to President Donald Trump.“‘The Administration thanks Dr. Schadlow for her service and leadership in crafting the President’s ‘America First’ National Security Strategy,” White House spokesman Raj Shah said in a statement. “The strategy has set a strong foundation going forward for protecting the homeland, promoting American prosperity, preserving peace through strength and advancing American influence. We wish Nadia and her family the best.”
