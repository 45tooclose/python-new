
Exclusive: Blockchain Firm Coinify Onboards Tradeworks Ex-CEO Mikael Breinholst Mikael will help bridge the gap between emerging blockchain and ‎traditional ‎finance industries.    Aziz Abdel-Qader  |  Executive Moves ( Executives )  |   Monday, 23/04/2018 | 18:58 GMT   Photo: iStockShare this articleCoinify, the Danish blockchain payment service provider, has ‎onboarded Mikael Breinholst, former CEO and Co-Founder of Tradeworks, to lead ‎the development efforts of its trading platform.‎In this role, Mr. Breinholst is responsible for managing the expansion of the business by ‎deploying best practices from the online trading world into development projects and ‎investment opportunities. His focus will be on shaping the firm’s relationships with industry ‎entities to accelerate trading business, as well as securing customers and white-label clients ‎to Coinify’s trading platform.‎Having spent twenty years within ‎the financial, media and telecom industries, Mikael is well positioned to play a key role in Coinify’s ‎transformation. He brings a wealth of experience for the Copenhagen-‎headquartered startup with ‎the mission to bridge the gap between emerging blockchain and ‎traditional finance industries. ‎Mikael BreinholstMr. Breinholst will help Coinify examine key benefits it ‎can obtain from mainstream financial markets and then offer ‎recommendations for aligning its established standards to the ‎cryptocurrency trading space.‎Prior to co-founding fintech company Tradeworks in 2013, Mikael was Global Product Manager and Head of Product ‎Communications at Saxo Bank, a tenure that ‎provided him with deep industry insight into ‎the FX brpkerage sector.As Finance Magnets reported last week, he ‎was replaced by Thomas Pedersen who was appointed CEO of Tradeworks after having been an early investor in the company since 2015.Established in 2014, Coinify serves global payment service ‎providers, online businesses, physical shops and individuals with easy ‎access to blockchain currencies through its money exchange services.‎‎Commenting on his appointment, Mikael Breinholst said: “I have been following Coinify for a several years and have watched them successfully expand ‎within the payment processing and trading space. I am proud to join a fantastic team of ‎talented and dedicated people, and I look forward to help drive Coinify to the forefront of the ‎virtual currency trading space.”Coinify CEO and Co-Founder, Mark Højgaard, ‎added: “It is very exciting to welcome ‎Mikael to the team.
